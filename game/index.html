The Flagship Game

One World. Every Table.
Nothing Resets.

You walk up to a table with up to three friends, tap in, and become a company of slightly doomed heroes. For the next fifteen minutes you're fighting through the Understair — a world beneath the town, a hundred levels deep, full of skeletons, treasure, terrible bargains, and things much larger than you.

Then your run ends — in glory or face-down — and here's the unusual part: the world keeps your mark.

How It Works

One run a day

Every player gets one free run per day. That's the whole price. It makes each run matter — and it means everyone else is watching.

A shared descent

The whole community is digging through the same hundred levels over a season. Nobody clears a level alone: every run moves a meter, and when it crosses the line, the level falls — for everyone, permanently. The number on the wall is the town's number.

The Ledger

Every deed is recorded by name, in a permanent public history written by the game's narrator. Land the killing blow, take a curse for the common good, get eaten by something ridiculous — it's in the book forever.

Seasons

Every few months the world ends — climactically — and reforges: new map, new secrets, new legends to be first to find. Glory carries forward. Power doesn't. Every season is a fresh start.

A world made of here

The realm is built from the real folklore and landmarks of the towns around the arcade. If you're local, you'll recognize things — and some secrets will only make sense to someone who knows the area.

The tone, for the record: the stakes are real, the world is ridiculous. Real heroism, talking skeletons, comedic deaths, a narrator who is not impressed with you.

Status

In development, honestly and in public. The combat prototype is playable (all circles and rectangles, on purpose), the persistent world pipeline is running, and you can watch the development world right now. The whole build is documented in the devlog. Doors — and the real Season 1 — open with the arcade in 2027.
